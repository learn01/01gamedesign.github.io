Topics we will go over during the camp duration


Server Operation

Learn how to setup your own private server that allows you to invite your friends to play. The skills learned here are applicable to networking and server administration.

Redstone Circuits

Learn how to use Redstone to wire up devices that react to the world. The skills learned here are applicable to digital circuits.

World Building

Learn how to design a world by building a map together with a team. The skills learned here are applicable to project planning and teamworking.

Command Programming

Learn how to use command blocks to create custom mobs and objectives. The skills learned here are applicable to computer science.

Minecamp FAQ


 
Do students need Minecraft?
The camp does not provide Minecraft accounts for use. Please purchase a Minecraft account for your PC/Mac/Linux laptop.
 
Do students need a laptop?
Please bring a laptop capable of running minecraft. We will not provide devices.
 
Do students need prior experience?
No prior experience is needed for the camp but learning will be tailored to each student’s progress level to ensure skills acquisition and increase confidence with STEAM topics.
 
How are students supervised?
The camp will have 2 full-time instructors with a maximum of 25 students. Instructors are trained in safety procedures including CPR, injury or allergic reaction protocols, and always have a copy of the camper’s emergency contacts with them.
 
Do I need to worry about my kid becoming a mad scientist?
After this camp…probably.
 
How do I pay?
A $25 deposit is required to reserve your spot through our online form. The remainder may be paid via check, cash, or our online payment system.
